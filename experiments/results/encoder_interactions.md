## Analysis (hand-written)

### Conclusions

1. **Interaction-aware encoders do not change the real-data picture: 4/4
   datasets, `identity` stays best.** `torch_mlp` is the better of the two
   on real data (california 0.4634 vs identity 0.4594 is within seed noise;
   house_sales/diamonds/adult are clearly behind) and shows the familiar
   Phase 14 overfit signature: lowest train metric, highest test metric,
   despite validation early stopping engaging at 11-29 of 30 epochs.
   `cross` is worse and *unstable* — on diamonds its residual-correlated
   pair selection produced a 0.1349 outlier on seed 0 (vs 0.0995 on seed 1),
   i.e. full-train correlation selection can latch onto noise pairs.
2. **The interaction hypothesis is real but already served by the router.**
   On `interaction_mix`, where the target is dominated by a feature product,
   both encoders beat `identity` decisively (cross 0.4598, torch_mlp 0.5367
   vs identity 0.6147) — representation-conditioned leaves *can* carry
   cross-feature structure the router would need many splits to emulate.
   On the real datasets that capacity buys nothing: whatever interactions
   matter there, axis-aligned routing already captures them, and the
   encoder only adds variance.
3. **This closes the second (and last planned) learned-encoder hypothesis
   from Phase 14.** Per-feature structure (Phases 13/14/14b) and
   cross-feature structure (this phase) both lose to `identity` on real
   tabular data under properly regularized pretraining. The conclusion is
   now well-supported: on typical tabular targets the raw-feature router
   plus identity-linear leaves is the right default, and learned encoders
   are specialist tools for targets with known structure that trees route
   poorly — smooth oscillations (`torch_periodic`) or dominant products
   (`cross` / `torch_mlp`).
4. **Defaults unchanged**: `identity` remains the default encoder; `cross`
   and `torch_mlp` ship as opt-in specialists. No estimator default moves
   on this evidence.

### Caveats

Two seeds; the california torch_mlp delta (+0.004) is within seed std.
`cross` uses full-train correlation for pair selection (no holdout), which
the diamonds outlier shows is a real weakness — a selection holdout would
likely stabilize it but was not tested. Only default widths
(hidden_dim=64, out_dim=16, n_pairs=16) were tested; no sweep.
