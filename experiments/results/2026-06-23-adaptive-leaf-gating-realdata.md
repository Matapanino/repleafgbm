## Analysis (verdict: KEEP_EXPERIMENTAL)

Focus arms: `constant`, `embedded_linear`, `adaptive` (LOO gate), `adaptive_insample`
(diagnostic baseline). Across california / house_sales / diamonds / adult (5 seeds),
`adaptive` tracks the better of `constant` and `embedded_linear` and is never the worst of
the three — but **every adaptive-vs-baseline difference is well inside one standard
deviation**: no decisive ≥1σ win or loss. On adult (binary) `adaptive` (0.2967) sits between
`constant` (0.2958) and `embedded_linear` (0.2976) within noise and beats `adaptive_insample`
(0.2984), consistent with the leverage correction mattering most on the noise-absorption
case; the `m=0.05` sweep arm (0.2958) closes the gap to constant. Fit time is ~1.3–1.8×
`embedded_linear` (the extra LOO pass).

**Verdict: KEEP_EXPERIMENTAL.** The evidence supports `adaptive` as an opt-in, robust
per-leaf hedge between constant and embedded-linear ridge leaves — within-noise no-worse than
the better baseline — **not** a general, statistically separated accuracy improvement. This
is per-leaf model *selection*, not jointly-trained leaf embeddings. Default stays
`embedded_linear`; `leaf_gate="insample"` is a diagnostic, not a recommendation. No default
change is warranted on this evidence.
