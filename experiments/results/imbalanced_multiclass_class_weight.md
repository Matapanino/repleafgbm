## Analysis

**Verdict: `class_weight='balanced'` works as intended — it raises balanced
accuracy at no meaningful cost to overall accuracy, and the gain scales with
how hard minority recall is.**

- On the harder, overlapping `sklearn_imbalanced` problem (priors
  0.7/0.2/0.07/0.03, `class_sep=0.9`, `flip_y=0.03`) balanced accuracy improves
  **+0.0318** (0.7712 → 0.8030, ≈ +3.2 points, ~0.8σ over the seed spread)
  while accuracy is statistically unchanged (+0.0012). This is the textbook
  imbalance trade — minority-class recall up, majority barely touched — and
  confirms the weight is reaching the split gains and leaf values, not just the
  init score.
- On the well-separated `blobs_skewed` problem the effect is small (+0.0010
  balanced accuracy) because both classifiers already recover every class at
  ≈0.98 recall; there is little minority recall left to buy. The −0.0005
  accuracy change is within noise.

**Mechanism check (consistent with the design):** weighting is folded into
`g, h` before the histogram (ADR 0004), so the lever acts on routing and leaf
fits, not only `F_0`. The accuracy-neutral, balanced-accuracy-positive pattern
is what that predicts.

**Recommendation:** keep the default `class_weight=None` (no evidence to change
a default, and on easy/balanced data the lever is a near-no-op). Document
`class_weight='balanced'` + `eval_metric="balanced_accuracy"` as the
recommended recipe for imbalanced multiclass targets — already done in the
estimator docstring and docs/roadmap.md (Phase 28). Possible follow-up: pair
with `label_smoothing` and/or per-class custom weights on a real imbalanced
benchmark (e.g. an OpenML long-tailed dataset) to confirm the trade holds
off-synthetic.
