## Analysis and conclusions (2026-06-11)

**Question.** Phase 0.5 benchmarking found `encoder="plr"` (then: n_bins=8,
projected to max_leaf_emb_dim=32) clearly worse than identity embeddings.
Is the cause the random projection, the PLR basis itself, or regularization?

**Findings.**

1. **The random projection is the primary culprit.** At fixed n_bins=4,
   removing the projection improves RMSE on piecewise_linear from 0.499
   (proj=8) / 0.428 (proj=32) to **0.396 (off)**; the same ordering holds on
   friedman1. Projecting to 8 dims is worst everywhere. Gaussian mixing
   destroys the per-feature, axis-aligned structure that the leaf-local ridge
   needs, and no l2 setting rescues it.
2. **Too many bins is the secondary culprit.** n_bins=4 beats 8 and 16 in
   every matched configuration. High-dimensional PLR without projection
   collapses the linear-leaf fraction (16 bins: linear% 0.17–0.25 on
   piecewise_linear) because leaves rarely have `emb_dim + 2` samples — the
   model silently degrades toward constant leaves, and what remains overfits
   (friedman1, bins=16 proj=off: RMSE 1.39–1.60, the worst results in the
   whole grid).
3. **PLR itself is sound.** With n_bins=4 and no projection it is the best
   configuration on piecewise_linear (0.396 vs identity 0.409) — wider-bin
   local nonlinearity helps where the signal has smooth curvature inside
   regimes. On friedman1 identity stays best (1.132 vs best-PLR 1.157);
   PLR's clipped components cannot extrapolate and friedman1's signal is
   dominated by terms identity handles after routing.
4. **l2_leaf is dataset-dependent** (0.3 best on piecewise_linear, 3.0 best
   for PLR on friedman1); no default change is justified.
5. The old default combination (n_bins=8 → 80 dims → project to 32) sat in
   the worst quadrant of the grid: exactly the Phase 0.5 gap.

**Actions taken (defaults changed in code).**

- `SimplePLREncoder` default `n_bins`: 8 → **4**.
- `max_leaf_emb_dim` default: 32 → **64** (with ≤16 numerical features,
  default PLR now runs unprojected).
- A `UserWarning` is emitted whenever the random projection engages,
  pointing at this report and recommending fewer bins / a larger cap.
- `encoder="identity"` remains the overall default (still the most robust).

**Open questions for later phases.**

- Smarter dimension reduction than random projection: per-leaf feature
  selection, supervised projections, or grouped (per-feature) compression.
- PLR extrapolation: an unclipped linear tail (or appending the raw value)
  might combine PLR's local flexibility with identity's extrapolation.
- The fallback threshold `max(2*min_samples_leaf, d_z + 2)` couples leaf size
  to embedding width; per-leaf adaptive dimension is unexplored.

**Caveats.** Two synthetic datasets, two seeds, one model size
(num_leaves=16). Conclusions are directional, not final; rerun this script
after any change to leaf fitting or encoders.
