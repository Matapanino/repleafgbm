## Analysis and conclusions (2026-06-11)

**Questions.** (a) Does an appended linear term fix PLR's extrapolation
problem? (b) Does a PBLD-style frozen periodic encoder (RealMLP-inspired)
close the friedman1 gap or win on oscillatory signals?

**Findings.**

1. **The linear term helps PLR where PLR is the right tool.** On
   piecewise_linear, `plr4 + linear` is the best configuration overall
   (0.3948), beating both plain `plr4` (0.4062) and `identity` (0.4074),
   and it converges fastest (best_it 66). On friedman1 it is neutral
   (1.1849 vs 1.1827, within seed noise). Default `add_linear=True` is kept.
2. **The friedman1 gap is structural, not an extrapolation bug.** Adding the
   linear term did not close it. friedman1's dominant term is
   `10·sin(π·x1·x2)` — a two-feature *interaction*, which no per-feature
   frozen encoder can represent by construction. Capturing it is the
   router's job (deeper trees) or a future learned encoder's. `identity`
   remains the best encoder there (1.1282).
3. **Frozen random frequencies do not deliver PBLD's benefit.** The periodic
   encoder lost everywhere — *including* on periodic_mix (0.454 vs identity
   0.395), the signal designed for it. With `f ~ N(0, 1)` the sampled
   frequencies rarely match the signal's (sin(3x)), so the sinusoidal
   dimensions act as noise that the leaf ridge must suppress. PBLD works in
   RealMLP because frequencies are *learned*; the frozen approximation keeps
   the dimensionality cost but not the adaptivity. The encoder stays
   available as an experimental option (`encoder="periodic"`), and this
   result is the concrete motivation for the learned-encoder track
   (PyTorch encoders / encoder pretraining in docs/roadmap.md).
4. **n_bins=4 reconfirmed**: `plr8 + linear` is worse than `plr4 + linear`
   on all three datasets, consistent with plr_projection_gap.md.
5. **Routing already covers locally-smooth oscillations.** On periodic_mix,
   `constant` (0.3984) nearly matches the best — trees slice sin(3x) into
   near-linear pieces, leaving little for any encoder. A reminder that
   encoder gains require structure that survives within leaves.

**Defaults after this experiment.**

- `plr`: `n_bins=4`, `add_linear=True` (unchanged from the code as shipped
  in this phase; now evidence-backed).
- Default encoder remains `identity` — never worse than second place on any
  dataset tested so far.
- `periodic` ships as opt-in/experimental with this caveat in its docs.

**Open questions.**

- Learned (PyTorch) encoders: do trained frequencies recover PBLD's wins?
  This is now the highest-value encoder question.
- `frequency_scale` was fixed at 1.0; a sweep might salvage some frozen
  performance but cannot fix the adaptivity problem in principle.
- Interaction-aware representations (e.g. small random feature *products*)
  as a cheap frozen probe of the friedman1-style gap.

**Caveats.** Three synthetic datasets, two seeds, l2_leaf fixed at 1.0,
one model size. Directional conclusions; rerun after encoder changes.
