## Analysis and conclusions — Phase 13 update (2026-06-12, learned encoders)

The Phase 1b analysis below this section concluded that frozen random
frequencies do not deliver PBLD's benefit and predicted that *learned*
frequencies would. The prediction is confirmed decisively:

1. **Learning beats its frozen counterpart in all 9 family/dataset cells.**
   torch_periodic vs frozen periodic: 0.4997→0.3893 (piecewise),
   1.3000→1.2395 (friedman1), 0.4505→**0.3405** (periodic_mix);
   torch_plr vs plr4+linear: 0.4032→0.3933, 1.1847→**1.1655**,
   0.4482→0.3871.
2. **torch_periodic is the best configuration overall on 2 of 3 datasets**,
   including periodic_mix — the dataset where the frozen version *lost to
   constant leaves* (0.4505 vs 0.3984). Learned frequencies turn the same
   architecture from worst-in-family to a 13% win over identity (0.3405 vs
   0.3933). This closes the Phase 1b question: PBLD's value lives in the
   adaptivity of its frequencies, and supervised pretraining on the initial
   Newton residual recovers it within the frozen-at-boost-time rule.
3. **The friedman1 interaction wall stands** (identity 1.1282 still best
   there): per-feature encoders, learned or not, cannot represent
   `sin(pi * x1 * x2)` — consistent with the Phase 1b structural analysis.
   torch_plr (1.1655) is nonetheless the best non-identity encoder yet.
4. **Learned encoders also converge fastest** (best_it 43-103 vs 66-219),
   i.e. better representations need fewer trees.
5. **Defaults**: unchanged — torch is an optional extra, so `identity`
   remains the default encoder. Documented guidance: with
   `pip install "repleafgbm[torch]"`, `encoder="torch_periodic"` is the
   recommended first choice for regression with suspected smooth/oscillatory
   within-region structure.

**Design notes validated in tests**: torch is needed only at fit time
(transform and serialization are NumPy; saved models predict without
torch); pretraining target is the Newton residual at the initial score, so
fit-then-freeze keeps the stage-wise additive analysis intact;
determinism holds (same seed, same learned parameters).

**Follow-ups**: binary-task evaluation of learned encoders (the Phase 12
route), interaction-aware learned features (pairwise inputs), and encoder
pretraining epochs/lr as searchable hyperparameters.
