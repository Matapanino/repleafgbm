## Analysis (hand-written)

### Conclusions

1. **Regularization does not change the real-data picture: 4/4 datasets,
   identity stays best.** On every real dataset the regularized and
   unregularized arms are statistically indistinguishable (deltas ≤0.003,
   within the seed std of the same row), and both lose to `identity` by the
   same margins Phase 14 measured. Validation early stopping engages
   exactly as designed — pretraining stops at 14-21 of 30 epochs — and it
   makes no difference to test accuracy.
2. **This closes the Phase 14 follow-up and confirms the architecture-level
   reading.** The overfit signature is *not* an artifact of an
   under-regularized pretraining loop: stopping at the pretraining-validation
   optimum still yields representations that do not transfer. What the
   learned per-feature encoders extract from the initial Newton residual on
   these targets is structure the trees already route on, plus noise. The
   remaining encoder direction on real data is *interaction-aware* features
   (cross-feature blocks), not better per-feature training.
3. **The synthetic win survives regularization intact** (periodic_mix:
   torch_periodic 0.3430 regularized vs 0.3446 unregularized vs identity
   0.3933, reproducing Phase 13's 0.3405/0.3933) — early stopping at ~18
   epochs does not destroy the genuinely-useful learned frequencies, so the
   guard is safe for the encoder's specialist use case.
4. **Defaults: regularization stays on** (`weight_decay=1e-3`,
   `val_fraction=0.15`, `patience=5`). Equal accuracy everywhere, 30-50%
   fewer pretraining epochs, and a principled guard for unseen targets.
   The Phase 14 guidance is unchanged: `identity` first on real tabular
   data; torch encoders are specialist tools for known smooth/oscillatory
   per-feature structure.

### Caveats

Two seeds; sub-0.005 differences are within seed noise. The unregularized
arm re-measures Phase 14 under the new code path (AdamW with
weight_decay=0 is mathematically Adam, but the batch-order RNG stream
differs), so its numbers shift by ~0.001-0.003 from the Phase 14 table —
the conclusions are unaffected. Only the conservative default knob values
were tested; no sweep.
